04 · Voice

Coach's voice. Not engineer's.

Speak like a thoughtful assistant coach who's already watched the film. Specific over generic. Quiet over hype. Numbers when they matter, words when they don't.

i.

Specific, not splashy

"Opponent loses the cross 64% on second serve return" — not "AI-powered insights at your fingertips." If a real coach wouldn't say it, neither do we.

ii.

Calm under pressure

No alerts that scream. No animations that fidget. The product earns trust by being quiet — the way a good film room is quiet — and surfacing the right answer when asked.

iii.

For the bench, not the timeline

Our copy is read between practices, not in a feed. Sentences can breathe. Numbers can sit. The reader is a coach with a clipboard, not a scroll.

05 · Icons

Lucide. Restrained.

Locked on Lucide — already in package.json via shadcn/ui, MIT-licensed, 1500+ glyphs. Never hand-roll an icon if Lucide has it. Override stroke and caps for brand fit.

Stroke weight: 1.75

Lucide default is 2. We override to 1.75 — slightly lighter lines pair with Newsreader and the rest of the editorial pacing. Never go below 1.5; readability suffers at 16px.

Caps and joins

stroke-linecap="round" and stroke-linejoin="round" across the board — same treatment as our court overlays and bar caps. Never square; reads industrial.

Color via currentColor

Always inherit from the parent. Set the parent's color, not the icon's stroke. One icon adapts across ink, court, clay, and any state without overrides.

Outline only — no filled glyphs

Lucide doesn't ship filled icons by default. Keep it that way. For "selected" states, use a colored background behind the outline icon, not a filled glyph.
